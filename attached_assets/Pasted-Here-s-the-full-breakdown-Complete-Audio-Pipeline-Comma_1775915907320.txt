KEY OBSERVATIONS FOR YOUR AUDIO PERSON
The compressor ratio is set to 1:1 in the live config — that means zero dynamic range compression. Quiet signals stay quiet. This is probably the single biggest reason for low volume at distance. Should be at least 3:1 or 4:1 for radio voice.

The LP filter coefficients have been modified — B0+B1+B2 = 1.44 in the live config (should sum to ~0.62 for unity gain at the passband). This means the filter is adding gain at some frequencies but the overall shaping may be wrong.

AGC is disabled on the T320 — the Android hardware automatic gain control is explicitly turned off. This means the raw mic level from the T320 hardware is what you get, with no automatic amplification of quiet input. The T320's mic is designed for close-range PTT use.

The desktop Playback Amplifier toggle works but hits the -3 dB limiter — at 150% incoming + 2x amplifier = 3x total gain, but the limiter has a -3 dBFS threshold with 20:1 ratio, so anything above -3 dB gets crushed. This makes the amplifier less noticeable when incoming volume is already high.

End-to-end latency budget: T320 pre-buffer + Opus encode (20ms frame) + UDP transit + server pacing (20ms interval) + browser reorder buffer (up to 90ms flush) + browser pre-buffer (1000ms initial / 500ms re-prime) + Web Audio processing = roughly 1.1–1.5 seconds first-frame latency.
